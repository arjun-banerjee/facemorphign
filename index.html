Face Morphing

Overview:

Here, we implement face morphing using only linear algebra — no ML! Then, we use it for cool things, like making GIFs, finding mean population faces, and making caricatures, such as making neutral expression people smile! Then, we try to explore the results of modifying the algorithm, such as trying out different morph techniques (sigmoid and logistic) and changing demographics.

Defining Corresponding Points & Mean Faces

Because everyone’s facial structures are different, it is necessary to find identify where key facial features are before beginning starting morphing. To do this, we select 38 different points on the face, and then perform Delaunay triangulation to capture triangular regions between the points.

We then compute the mid way faces using the following algorithm:

  1. Compute the average points of the features with 12(P1+P2)\frac{1}{2}(P_{1} + P_{2})21​(P1​+P2​)﻿ for each point
  1. Find the Delaunay triangulation of these points
  1. For each triangle, compute the affine transformation that sends the given triangle to the average warp triangle
  1. Compute the inverse warp using the inverse of the affine transformation, using polygonal mask and linear interpolation.
  1. Add the images by cross dissolving with a factor of 12\frac{1}{2}21​﻿

Part 4: Mean Face of a Population

Using the FEI dataset, which consists of 200 photographs of Brazilians smiling and neutral face, we are able to create the average population faces — both smiling and not! Using their labelings, we are able to create the average point set and triangulation for smiling, neutral, and combined smiling and neutral, and warp people to these facial structures!

We can morph faces, add them, and then find their average to find the mean face of the Brazilian (sample) population smiling, neutral, and combined:

We can now morph myself to the average Brazillian and vice versa.

Why so much distortion? There’s a few reasons

  1. The average Brazilian seems to have no hair — likely because there is a blending of many hairstyles. I have a LOT of hair.
  1. The average Brazilian photo leave a cutoff right above their forehead. Whereas my points extend to my afro. As such, the brazil to Arjun warp has to interpolate on points that don’t exist by expanding the Brazilian’s forehead into triangles that represent my afro.
  1. The average Brazilian is smiling — I was not. And they smile with teeth! As such, their mouth is significantly bigger than mine. Such happy folk : )
  1. The Brazilians were closer to the camera than me, so their heads are likely bigger.
